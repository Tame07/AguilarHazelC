Sustainable Development Goals

Sustainable Development Goals, as established by the United Nations, are based on the idea that economic growth, social inclusion, and environmental protection are interconnected and essential for the well-being of individuals and societies. Its global objectives consist of 17 goals that aim to end poverty, fight inequality, tackle climate change, promote prosperity, protect the planet, address social needs like education, health, and job opportunities, ensure that no one is left behind, and many others. This is applied to countries all over the world, regardless of different development statuses. There is also monitoring and reviewing of these goals using a set of global indicators, like the Secretary-General that prepares an annual progress report to inform the follow-up and review process.

Gender Equality

As part of the 17 goals, Sustainable Development Goal No. 5, gender equality, aims to achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls. The United Nations considers gender equality to be a fundamental human right and a vital factor in building a peaceful, prosperous, and sustainable world. And to make it possible, this goal pursues the main goal of real and sustained gender equality in all aspects of women and girls’ lives, which includes ending gender disparities, eliminating violence against women and girls’ lives, eliminating early and forced marriage, securing equal participation and opportunities for leadership, and universal access to sexual and reproductive rights. If this is finally achieved, then a society of gender equality can build a future of impartiality.

SDG

Gender equality is significant in a wide variety of fields. Aside from promoting equal access to opportunities, resources, and rights, it also contributes to economic, social, political, cultural, environmental, technological, and legal development, ensuring that different genders in societies thrive in more sustainable and inclusive ways. It also protects intellectual, emotional, physical, social, occupational, financial, environmental, and spiritual health all along. By breaking the barriers that separate individuals’ genders, everyone can build valuable and full potential in creating a peaceful and better world to live in. As gender equalities are unlocked, there’s the locking of deep-rooted biases and structural barriers towards inequalities. It is a cause that resonates across all sectors of society, making it a crucial pillar for a fair and prosperous future. Gender equality—a human right, an urgent need, and a beneficial one. So as you treat everyone equally, you are also saving lives eventually.
